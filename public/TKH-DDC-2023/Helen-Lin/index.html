    Aviation High School:

    Founded in 1936, Aviation Career and Technical Education High School is a CTE school that is certified by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) whichtrains students to become Aviation Maintenance Technicians (AMTS) via preparation to take the Airframe of Powerplant Qualifying Tests. Every day, students spend 3 to 4 periods in a hangar designed room. This class is called "shop". Students have rotations which introduce them to topics in the FAA-H-8083-30A textbook. Through these shop classes, which are usually taught by A&P certified alumni of the school, students become famirliarized with the textbook information and the mechanical practices of maintaining aircrafts and their components. By completing the 4 or optional 5 years of shop, students are able to test for their licenses.
